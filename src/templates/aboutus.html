About Zapp

Bitcoin's User Friendly Face

Started in 2018 by a developer passionate about the mission of tackling Bitcoin's usability and scaling issues, hindering mass adoption. Currently working at the largest cryptocurrency exchange based in Japan, Singapore and Ho Chi Minh. A member of the crypto community, not the financial establishments.

Zapp is an application which allows users to send Btc across the internet with simply a username. Zapp is what Venmo is to the current banking system. Your current Btc wallet will serve as your personal "Bank", whilst Zapp functions as a way to spend your Btc easily, securely and for no fee's. No Financial Institutions, No Banks, No Credit Cards, No Fiat, No Fee's. No BS, simple BTC payments.

How It Works

Step 1: Make an Account. No Email required, Just a creative, available username.

Step 2: Deposit BTC from your current wallet into the Zapp Account. Current deposits for the beta are topped at 100usd for security purposes.

Step 3: Send BTC to whoever you want for free using just their username. Attach a message to each transaction too.

Step 4: When you want to withdraw out of your account, just click withdraw, enter any Btc address, and get your Btc withdrawn instantly. No Fee's on withdrawals to external addresses differentiates Zapp from other services.

Advantages of Zapp

Bitcoin allows you to be your own bank. No threats of governmental seizure and no need to rely upon the decisions of those placed in power. With Bitcoin we are responsible for our money.

However, rather than a store of value, Bitcoins survival hinges on its use in p2p internet payments. Bitcoin at its present state is too costly, slow, inefficient and complex for most users to use. Even with the addition of the lightning network a user friendly interface is needed without comprimising the p2p, private, bank-less free nature of btc. This is where Zapp comes into play.

Send Btc to anyone over the internet without having to know them personally. Your username is the only thing you need to accept payments.
