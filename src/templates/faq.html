FAQ and Misconceptions

How is Zapp Free?
I currently have a day job, so I can fund Zapps development considering I am the only developer. If I decide to recruit a team then I will have to rely upon community donations. Zapp is not built solely for the purpose of making profit, but to address a key problem hindering mass adoption of Bitcoin. Fee free transactions are key to ensure the power rests with the users.

Is Zapp Safe?
In order to address concerns about fund safety, I decided that I would limit the maximum amount you can hold in your Zapp account. This would put less of your Btc to a potential risk. In addition during periods of code testing, funds will all be withdrawn to your account.

Settling Concerns?
Either send an email or contact me directly through any of the links on the contacts page.

Doesn't Zapp centralise Bitcoin?
Although Zapp can be seen as a way of centralising Bitcoin, this is not the full picture. The power of Bitcoin is in its ability to allow individuals to act as their own financial institutions. As long as your main Bitcoin wallet is held securely, there is no way Zapp can access your funds or control your transactions. In fact Zapp does not even require any personal information from you. Your entire online Bitcoin identity is not linked to a bank, but merely an anonymous username. In my view, a consumer facing application is necessary if Bitcoin is to be adopted on a wider scale. User friendly usernames and the ability to attach messages can allow for previously unexplored opportunities. I do not make any profit from Zapp as I am self funding this project. I truly want for Bitcoin to succeed and am doing my part to help.
